January 19, 2017

Data visualization is high among my passions;

A combination of many things I love, and as a culmination of experience, visualization comes naturally (or rather as the product of certain skills.)

As an artist, potter, painter, working at the Metropolitan Museum was living a certain dream. Walking behind the scenes, underground, seeing laboratories, learning and participating in the history of that great institution and repository of knowledge, amazed me.

I left the MET to learn coding at the Start Up Institute. (originated from Harvard Media Labs in Boston, is now here in NYC, and is a sister organization of the acclaimed incubator: TechStars.) After taking up development and coding, I had, a year ago, the distinct honor, teaching visual design at General Assembly (a trendy organization with a reputation of its own).

I love information architecture, which is user experience and user interfaces, graphics, interaction and other sheer things among data, supporting technical representations of clear meaning, utility and value.

I enjoy using metrics, and at my current work now often keep records which are meaningful, maps, graphs, only for me, and something I construct using of data I personal (or rather manually write as comma separated values (CSV or other data formats) for fun, and to use.

And crucially I am a fan of Drupal, (arguably the most modern, open-source, and highly proliferant, among platforms.) Drupal, highly secure, is extendable to many, many data systems, such as the NYC subway kiosk, an interactive and asynchronous map; and it is powered by an incredible and free, open source, technology.


- Jordan Lafland
